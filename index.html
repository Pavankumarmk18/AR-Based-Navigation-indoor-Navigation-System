AR Indoor Navigation turns a photo of your building's floor plan into a walkable map, then guides people to any room using a compass-driven AR arrow overlaid on their phone's live camera feed — no GPS, no beacons, no app store required.

02 / PRINT CHECKPOINTS

Generate a QR code per pin and stick them at doorways — that's how the app knows where you are.

03 / WALK WITH AR

Open the camera. A HUD arrow rotates live to point toward the next checkpoint.

Why no SLAM / markerless tracking?

True markerless AR (ARCore/ARKit-style world tracking) needs native device APIs that aren't reliably available inside a browser. AR Indoor Navigation uses a method that is reliable across phones: a pre-mapped floor-plan graph, the phone's magnetometer (compass) for arrow orientation, and QR checkpoints for accurate position resets as you pass them. It's a deliberate engineering trade-off — documented in the README, along with BLE-beacon and native-SLAM as future extensions.
